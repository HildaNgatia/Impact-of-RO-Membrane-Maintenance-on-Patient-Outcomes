# Impact-of-RO-Membrane-Maintenance-on-Patient-Outcomes

## Exploring the Impact of RO Membrane Maintenance on Hemodialysis Patient Outcomes: A Retrospective Study

This retrospective observational study examined the relationship between reverse osmosis (RO) membrane maintenance frequency and clinical outcomes in hemodialysis patients across 34 healthcare facilities over a 16-month period (January 2024 to April 2025). The analysis of 544 patient-months of data revealed signi cant associations between membrane maintenance practices and key clinical indicators, providing evidence-based insights for dialysis unit quality improvement protocols.

### Key Findings:
- More frequent RO membrane changes were signi cantly associated with reduced infection rates, hospital admissions, and mortality
- Each additional membrane change was associated with a 5.1% reduction in infection risk and 10.4% reduction in mortality risk
- Older membrane age correlated with poorer clinical outcomes across all measured parameters
- Water quality parameters (TDS and conductivity) showed signi cant relationships with patient outcomes
